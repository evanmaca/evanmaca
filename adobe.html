ADOBE PROJECTS


Software: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator

Below is a collection of some of my creative works on Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator!

Self Portrait


This self portrait was painted in Adobe illustrator. Essentially all I did was use the pain brush tool to paint over the original image, giving it a new and unique texture. I also chose some bright and vibrant pastel colors to really make the image pop out to the human eye.



Anti-Racism Project


These collection of images I designed in photoshop were made in response to the recent spike of racism and violence against Asian Americans. The objective of this assignment was to take known historical paintings and then recreate them in a modern context. I took inspiration from some portrait paintings done by a German artist named Hans Holbein, and recreated those portraits with myself as a model.

This collection tells a fictional but relevant narrative about an Asian youth who may be going through some feelings of internalized racism. The young man is trying hard to prove his “Americanness” by attempting to appeal to other westerners through different acts and statements.

From right to left is the progression of trying to appeal to a western audience. The final photo on the left is the final transformation, where he is proud to be Asian and doesn’t feel obligated to prove anything to anyone. He is proud of his heritage has nothing to hide.




Face Morph Project


This exercise was done in photoshop. The objective of this assignment was to take photos of my classmates and then morph their facial features amongst each other to create something fun and ridiculous. The process used various processes such as masking and blending to make the various facial features mesh well together.
