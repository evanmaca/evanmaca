IKEA WEBSITE REDESIGN


Role: UX UI Designer, Researcher
Duration: 3 Weeks
Software: Figma, Adobe Illustrator

Founded in 1943, IKEA is a Swedish multinational conglomerate that’s titled as one of the leaders in the worldwide furniture industry. Though commonly regarded as a physical shopping experience, IKEA has recently seen an increase in website traffic due to a pandemic-induced push towards online shopping.

In a rapidly growing e-commerce space, IKEA will need to keep up with its competitors and offer a seamless digital experience across all devices.

DISCOVER

Design Process


Double Diamond Design Process

For this project, I have followed the Double Diamond Design Process, working with the flow of: Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver.

Doing it this way allows me to research the challenge to define a problem statement, then design and prototype iteratively.


RESEARCH

Gathering Data and Empathizing


I studied participants to look for prominent shopping habits while walking through physical IKEA stores and browsing through the IKEA website.

It was discovered that there were two main types of IKEA shoppers: Product aware shoppers, and product unaware shoppers.


IKEA's Target Demographic


After doing secondary research, I observed that IKEA appeals towards Millennials.

The key takeaways from studying IKEA’s target demographic were:

  • Millennials embrace change. This generation of people grew up with insecurity in the marketplace and is accustomed to the rapid development of most industries.
  • There is a desire for personalized experiences. Millennials prefer experiences over material things and appreciate opportunities for interaction.
  • They gravitate toward purchases that are an expression of their personality and place value on products that fulfills their own emotional needs.
  • Millennials were more likely to share their purchases on social media which provides a good forum for discussion and endorsements.

Competitive Research


Amazon and Walmart were IKEA’s biggest online competitors in 2020. Amazon is a digital retailer that specialize in online shopping while IKEA and Walmart are more of a physical experience where people prefer to get their purchases in-store.

Walmart

Some highlights I found while browsing the Walmart online shopping experiences were:

  • Walmart’s homepage is very interactive, allowing users the opportunity to add individual and specific items into their cart, whereas IKEA’s homepage is more informational, showcasing their story, philsophy and catalogue. A blend of these two worlds would be suitable for the redesign.
  • By allowing users to directly add to cart from the home page, Walmart gives the opportunity for product unaware shoppers to browse through low cost items and add it into their basket accordingly
  • Walmart's products are organized based on deals, prices, categories, etc. This allows product aware shoppers to easily filter through items and reach their desired goal.





Some issues I found while exploring Walmart's website were:

There is a confusing measurement system on Walmart’s products. This needs to be avoided during the IKEA redesign, there needs to be a consistent measurement scheme. For example, Airpods are listed as $124.99 ($128.98/cu ft), the other items do not have a special unit of measure.


Amazon

Some highlights I found while browsing the Amazon online shopping experiences were:

  • Amazon provides recommended products to shoppers based on their browsing history. This feature may be very useful for product unaware shoppers
  • Amazon's categories are displayed using a grid-based layout. This allows the product aware shopper to filter through various products and arrive at the item they choose in an efficient manner.



Some issues I found while exploring Amazon's website were:

The Amazon grid-based display is overwhelming and needs to be avoided during the redesign.

Though the grid display organizes categories in a logical manner, the way Amazon presents it has a poor use of negative space and gives the user a feeling of crowdedness. The IKEA homepage redesign should adopt an F or Z layout to optimize negative space and reduce the feeling of a crowded display.

IKEA Website Analysis


The focus of IKEA’s homepage is informational, showcasing company values and new collections, rather than products. This allows both product aware and unaware shoppers to see company values and builds a sense of transparency between the corporation and the consumer.

IKEA provides services that some of its competitors do not provide - online planning, financing options, TaskRabbit assembly, kitchen services, credit card deals, etc - There should be lots of emphasis on these services, as these are things that online retailers like Amazon and Walmart do not have.





When looking at individual products on the IKEA website, I noticed a major flaw, there is no option to adjust the quantity of the item. This makes it difficult for shoppers who want to buy multiple copies of an item.
